How to read this screen

The page separates forecast accuracy, probability uncertainty, market economics, and operational risk.

1. Look at the distribution, not just the point.

A model can have a 74°F point estimate while still assigning meaningful probability to 75–76°F. The shaded mixture is what drives contract probabilities.

2. Compare p-trade with the required probability.

A positive difference means the weather probability is high enough for the active return hurdle at the current price—before disagreement and data gates.

3. Read the decision reason.

A large economic edge can still be blocked by a 4°F spread, stale source timestamp, invalid book sequence, insufficient residual history, or unverified settlement rule.
